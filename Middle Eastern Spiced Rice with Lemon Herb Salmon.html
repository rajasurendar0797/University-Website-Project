    • Middle Eastern Spiced Rice with Lemon Herb Salmon

    Middle Eastern Lunch Dinner Pescatarian Middle East Savory Aromatic Weekend Feast (3+ hours) Salmon

    Ingredients

    For the Spiced Rice:

    • 1 cup basmati rice
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
    • 1 tsp ground cumin
    • ½ tsp pepper
    • ½ tsp ground turmeric
    • ½ tsp allspice
    • ½ tsp salt
    • ¼ cup golden raisins
    • ¼ cup slivered almonds, toasted
    • 2 cups vegetable stock
    • 1 tbsp ghee (optional, for extra richness)

    For the Lemon Herb Salmon:

    • 4 salmon fillets (about 150g each)
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1 tsp dried dill
    • ½ tsp salt
    • ½ tsp black pepper
    • 1 tbsp olive oil (for searing)

    For the Marinated Salmon (overnight):

    • 4 salmon fillets (about 150g each)
    • 3 tbsp olive oil
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • 1 tbsp honey
    • 1 tsp ground cumin
    • ½ tsp ground turmeric
    • 1 tsp smoked paprika
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    For Garnish:

    • Fresh parsley, chopped
    • Lemon wedges

    Instructions

    1. Step 1: Marinate the Salmon (overnight or 3 hours before cooking)
      • In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, honey, cumin, turmeric, smoked paprika, garlic, salt, and pepper.
      • Place the salmon fillets in a shallow dish or a resealable bag and pour the marinade over the salmon. Ensure the fillets are coated evenly.
      • Refrigerate for at least 3 hours or overnight for deeper flavor infusion.

    2. Step 2: Prepare the Spiced Rice (2–3 hours) :
      • Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain well.
      • Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 5–7 minutes, until golden and fragrant.
      • Add the garlic, cinnamon, cumin, turmeric, allspice, and salt, and sauté for 1–2 minutes to release the aromas.
      • Add the drained rice to the pan, stirring to coat it in the spices. Pour in the vegetable stock and bring to a boil.
      • Once it boils, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes.
      • After 15 minutes, remove the pan from the heat and let it rest, covered, for 30 minutes. This will allow the rice to steam and fluff up.
      • While the rice rests, heat ghee in a separate pan and toast the slivered almonds until golden brown (about 3–5 minutes).
      • Fluff the rice with a fork and fold in the toasted almonds and golden raisins. Cover the rice and set aside to keep warm.

    3. Step 3: Sear the Salmon (about 30 minutes before serving):
      • Heat 1 tbsp of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
      • Remove the salmon from the marinade and gently pat it dry with a paper towel.
      • Add the salmon fillets to the hot skillet, skin-side down, and cook for 4–5 minutes on each side, until golden brown and cooked through.
      • Once cooked, remove the salmon from the skillet and let it rest for 5 minutes.

    4. Step 4: Serve the Feast (10 minutes):
      • Arrange a bed of the spiced rice on each serving plate.
      • Place a salmon fillet on top of the rice.
      • Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and lemon wedges on the side.

    5. Enjoy:
      • Enjoy your Middle Eastern-inspired pescatarian weekend feast with tender salmon, aromatic rice, and all the bold flavors of the region!
