    • Australian Grilled Barramundi with Mango Salsa

    Australian Asian Main Course Lunch Refreshing Keto Dairy-Free Healing Celebratory Quick recipes Seafood

    Ingredients

    • 2 barramundi fillets
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 mango, diced
    • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
    • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
    • 1 lime, juiced

    Instructions

    1. Prepare the Barramundi:
      • Rinse the barramundi fillets under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. This helps to ensure they cook evenly and the seasoning sticks.
      • Season the fillets on both sides with salt and pepper to taste. Be sure to season evenly to enhance the flavor of the fish.
      • If using a grill, preheat it to medium heat (around 180-200°C or 350-400°F). If using a grill pan or skillet, preheat it on the stove over medium-high heat.

    2. Prepare the Mango Salsa:
      • Mango: Peel the mango and slice the flesh away from the pit. Cut the mango into small cubes (about 1/4 inch in size). Try to select a ripe mango for sweetness and texture.
      • Red Onion: Peel and finely chop 1/2 red onion. Be sure the pieces are small to allow the salsa to have a uniform texture.
      • Cilantro: Rinse the cilantro under cold water and remove the stems. Chop the cilantro leaves roughly.
      • Lime: Cut the lime in half and juice it, ensuring no seeds get into the juice. Fresh lime juice adds a bright acidity that balances the sweetness of the mango.
      • In a mixing bowl, combine the diced mango, chopped red onion, and cilantro.Add the lime juice and mix well. Taste the salsa and adjust seasoning with a little salt or pepper if needed. Set aside.

    3. Grill the Barramundi:
      • Once the grill or grill pan is preheated, place the barramundi fillets on the grill with the skin-side down (if the skin is still on).
      • Grill the fillets for about 4-5 minutes on the first side. Avoid moving the fish around to ensure it sears properly.
      • Carefully flip the fillets using a spatula or tongs, and cook for an additional 4-5 minutes on the other side. The fish should be opaque and flake easily when tested with a fork. If you’re using a grill with a lid, you can close it during cooking to ensure even heat distribution.
      • If you’re using a grill pan, you can reduce the heat to medium if the fish is browning too quickly.

    4. Serve:
      • Once the fish is cooked, transfer the barramundi fillets to serving plates.
      • Top each fillet with a generous amount of the mango salsa.
      • Garnish with additional cilantro or a lime wedge, if desired, for extra freshness and flavor.

    5. Enjoy:
      • Serve the grilled barramundi immediately while hot. Pair it with a side salad, steamed vegetables, or a light green salad for a refreshing, well-rounded meal.
